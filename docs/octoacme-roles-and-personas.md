# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers maintain clear, accessible project documentation and coordinate release notes and user-facing materials. They ensure that information is accurate, up-to-date, and discoverable.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain technical documentation, user guides, and API references
- Coordinate release notes with Product Managers and Developers
- Review PRs for documentation completeness and clarity
- Maintain documentation standards and style guides
- Ensure documentation accessibility and searchability

### Goals
- Deliver clear, consistent documentation that reduces support burden
- Ensure all features have appropriate documentation before release
- Maintain a single source of truth for project information

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews in PRs
- Release notes coordination meetings
- Regular syncs with Developers and Product Managers for content updates

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborates to understand technical implementation, review documentation PRs, and validate documentation during testing
- **Product Managers**: Coordinates on feature descriptions, user-facing announcements, and release notes
- **Support Lead**: Incorporates feedback on documentation gaps and common user questions
- **UX Designer**: Ensures documentation reflects design decisions and user experience

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, coach the team on agile principles, and help remove blockers. They enable smooth team operations and continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives
- Coach team members on agile practices and principles
- Identify and help resolve blockers and impediments
- Track team metrics (velocity, burndown) and foster transparency
- Shield the team from external disruptions

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement
- Ensure agile ceremonies are effective and time-boxed

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation of standups
- Sprint planning and retrospective sessions
- One-on-one coaching conversations
- Blocker escalation to Project Managers and Product Leads

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Partners to align on delivery timelines and escalate cross-team blockers
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates during backlog refinement and sprint planning
- **Developers**: Coaches on agile practices and helps remove technical impediments
- **Business Analyst**: Works together to ensure user stories are ready for sprint planning

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers design user experiences, create wireframes and prototypes, and advocate for customer needs throughout the product lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Define user flows and interaction patterns
- Collaborate on design systems and component libraries
- Validate designs with stakeholders and users

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Advocate for user needs in product decisions
- Ensure design consistency across the product

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- Prototype walkthroughs with Product Managers and Developers
- User research findings presentations
- Design specifications in PRs or design tools

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates on feature requirements, user needs, and design validation
- **Developers**: Works closely on implementation feasibility and design handoff
- **Business Analyst**: Partners to understand business requirements and translate into user experiences
- **Support Lead**: Incorporates user feedback and pain points into design iterations

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts elicit requirements, translate business goals into technical solutions, and document acceptance criteria. They bridge the gap between stakeholders and the delivery team.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct requirements gathering sessions with stakeholders
- Document functional and non-functional requirements
- Write detailed user stories with acceptance criteria
- Analyze business processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Validate solutions against business needs

### Goals
- Ensure clear, complete requirements that minimize rework
- Align technical solutions with business objectives
- Facilitate effective communication between business and technical teams

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and user stories
- Stakeholder interviews and workshops
- Acceptance criteria validation sessions
- Business process diagrams and data flow documentation

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborates to understand product vision and prioritize features
- **Developers**: Clarifies requirements and answers technical questions about business logic
- **Project Managers**: Coordinates on scope, timelines, and dependency management
- **UX Designer**: Works together to ensure designs meet business requirements and user needs

---

## Support Lead / Customer Advocate

### Role Summary
Support Leads represent user feedback, escalate critical support issues, and contribute insights to product development. They are the voice of the customer within the team.

### Responsibilities
- Collect and synthesize customer feedback from support channels
- Escalate high-impact issues to Product Managers and Engineering
- Contribute to post-release reviews with customer insights
- Identify documentation gaps based on support tickets
- Participate in beta testing and early access programs

### Goals
- Ensure customer pain points are addressed in product development
- Reduce support burden through proactive issue resolution
- Improve customer satisfaction and retention

### Typical Communication
- Weekly support metrics and trending issues reports
- Escalation emails for critical customer issues
- Retrospective participation with product insights
- Feature feedback based on customer requests

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Provides customer feedback to inform roadmap priorities
- **Developers**: Reports bugs and helps reproduce customer issues
- **Technical Writer**: Highlights documentation gaps and improvement areas
- **Business Analyst**: Shares customer use cases and requirements insights
